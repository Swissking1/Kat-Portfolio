Nomad Chair

fall '16

The Nomad Chair was an exploration of altering existing designs to meet custom user needs. During this exploration, I was spending sporadic time in studio while carrying up to three bags to my desk every day. I would often work at my desk without removing my backpack or tote bags to try and be as efficient as possible with my time. This chair is custom made for my measurements and my belongings --- a special object which shaped my interaction with the spaces I inhabited.

I think of The Nomad Chair as my first formalized human centered design process. While in previous projects I'd done iterative design reactively, The Nomad Chair was my first assignment in a studio class, characterized by feedback from my peers and mentors.

The Nomad Chair taught me the importance of using feedback from others to improve my desings. In my final critique, we discussed that while this project successfully meets my personal needs, design is often about accessibility to a variety of audiences. To move this project forward, I would explore more versatile and playful forms that invite interaction rather than designing to meet my needs alone.
